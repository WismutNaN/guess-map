Recognizing bollards can be key in GeoGuessr; look for unique features like Serbia's off-center red rectangle or Croatia's white-back design. The Czech Republic has fluorescent orange stripes, Ukrainian bollards appear weathered, and Turkey's resemble Australia with a thicker rectangle. Differentiation matters — note Finnish cylindrical shapes, Austrian dark reflectors, and the rare vertical rectangle on Italian and Albanian bollards.

Bollards Metas

Bangladeshi bollards look like chimneys and are painted in alternating red and white sections. Sometimes the bollards are shorter than this image and thus have less red and white sections.
🇧🇩 Bangladesh
Although bollards are relatively rare in the UK compared to some other European countries, they are rather unique looking and consist of black and white sections with a large red rectangle on top.
🇬🇧 United Kingdom (UK)
Bollards in Luxembourg are wedge-shaped with a grey rectangular reflector in the front and two circular reflectors in the rear.
🇱🇺 Luxembourg
In Ecuador, bollards are quite common, which is unique in Latin America. Bollards are either round with two red stripes or flat with two red reflectors on a black background. While various other styles of bollards can be found in Ecuador, they typically maintain a consistent color scheme and style.
🇪🇨 Ecuador
Hungarian bollards have a red rectangle on the front. The back side has the same rectangle but in white.
🇭🇺 Hungary
These white and yellow stone markers indicate how far away the nearest village, town or city is in Bhutan. In this example, the village of Dewathang is 1km away. The other side of the stone also has information about how far away the nearest village is for those travelling in the opposite direction.
🇧🇹 Bhutan
